Problem Statement

Given

Water is siphoned from a large tank through a constant-diameter hose. The outlet (point 3) is below the tank bottom, and the hose rises over a hill crest (point 2) at elevation \(H\).

Aside (cavitation): When the pressure of a liquid drops below its saturation (vapor) pressure, a phase change from liquid to vapor can generate vapor bubbles.

Determine the maximum hill elevation \(H\) such that cavitation does not occur at the crest.

Solution

Apply Bernoulli between point (2) and point (3):

\[ p_2 + \rho g z_2 + \frac{1}{2}\rho V_2^2 \;=\; p_3 + \rho g z_3 + \frac{1}{2}\rho V_3^2 \]

Because the hose diameter is constant, \(V_2=V_3\). Also, the outlet is a free jet so \(p_3=0\) (gage). Therefore,

\[ z_2 = z_3 - \frac{p_2}{\rho g} \]

With the tank bottom as the datum, \(z_2=H\) and \(z_3=-h_2\) where \(h_2\) is the outlet drop below the tank bottom. Thus,

\[ H = -h_2 - \frac{p_2}{\rho g} = -h_2 - \frac{p_2}{\gamma} \]
