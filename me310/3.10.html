Problem Statement

Known

Water at \(60^\circ\mathrm{F}\) is siphoned from a large tank through a constant-diameter hose. The outlet (point 3) is below the tank bottom, and the hose rises over a hill crest (point 2) at elevation \(H\).

At incipient cavitation, the crest absolute pressure is \[ p_{2,\mathrm{abs}} = 0.256~\mathrm{psia}. \]

Using atmospheric pressure, \[ p_2 = p_{2,\mathrm{abs}} - p_{\mathrm{atm}} = 0.256 - 14.7 = -14.44~\mathrm{psig}. \]

Find

Determine the maximum hill elevation \(H\) such that cavitation does not occur at the crest.

Analysis

Apply Bernoulli between point (2) and point (3):

\[ p_2 + \rho g z_2 + \frac{1}{2}\rho V_2^2 \;=\; p_3 + \rho g z_3 + \frac{1}{2}\rho V_3^2 \]

Because the hose diameter is constant, \(V_2 = V_3\). Also, the outlet is a free jet so \(p_3 = 0\) (gage). Therefore,

\[ z_2 = z_3 - \frac{p_2}{\rho g} \]

With the tank bottom as the datum, \(z_2 = H\) and \(z_3 = -h_2\). Thus,

\[ H = -h_2 - \frac{p_2}{\rho g} \]

For water at \(60^\circ\mathrm{F}\), the pressure corresponding to cavitation is the saturation pressure, \(p_{2,\mathrm{abs}} = p_{\mathrm{sat}} = 0.256~\mathrm{psia}\). Converting to gage pressure gives

\[ p_2 = p_{2,\mathrm{abs}} - p_{\mathrm{atm}} = 0.256 - 14.7 = -14.44~\mathrm{psig} \]

so the maximum allowable crest height becomes

\[ H = -h_2 - \frac{p_2}{\rho g}. \]
